Lena Borise

I am a Research Fellow at the Hungarian Research Centre for Linguistics. Before this, in 2019, I got a PhD in Linguistics from the Department of Linguistics at Harvard. My research lies at the intersection of syntax and phonology, with focus on understudied languages.

I am particularly interested in how prosody interacts with clause structure, and how information structure is reflected in syntax and prosody. In my dissertation, I investigated these topics in Georgian, a Kartvelian language spoken in the Caucasus.

I am also interested in the phonetics and phonology of stress and prosodic prominence. I have worked on the properties of stress in Georgian, Udmurt (Uralic), and the Malyja Aŭciuki (Малыя Аўцюкі) dialect of Belarusian (Slavic).
